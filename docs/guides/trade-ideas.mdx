---
title: Trade ideas & AI picks
description: "Surface Pear's statistical-arbitrage baskets and turn one into an analyzed, sized trade."
---

`pear_trade_ideas` exposes three streams of pair/basket ideas. Combined with `pear_pair_analytics`, an agent can go from "what should I look at?" to "here's a sized, mean-reverting entry" without you doing the legwork.

## The three categories

| Category | Source |
| --- | --- |
| `active` | Pairs/baskets the platform is actively trading |
| `watchlist` | Your saved watchlist |
| `ai-picks` | Pear's statistical-arbitrage engine ("Agent Pair") |

The tool returns a `baskets` array; passing `category` filters it to one stream.

## A full idea-to-trade flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pull the AI picks">
    > Call `pear_trade_ideas` with `category: "ai-picks"`. List each basket with its legs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a candidate and gather prices">
    Choose one basket. Fetch aligned price history for its long and short legs (from your own data source — the analytics tool is data-source agnostic).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quantify the entry">
    > Run `pear_pair_analytics` on the candidate's long vs short prices. Is `|z| ≥ 2`? What's the hedge ratio?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Size and (optionally) execute">
    Use `beta` to size the legs market-neutral. If you've enabled trading, hand off to [a guarded trade](/guides/guarded-trade) with `pear_open_position` + `confirm: true`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example prompt

> Show me Pear's AI-pick baskets. For the one with the highest historical correlation between legs, run pair analytics and tell me whether the spread is extended enough to enter, and what weights would make it market-neutral.

## Combine with market context

Cross-reference ideas with live market state:

> Pull `ai-picks`, then `pear_get_markets` filtered to those assets sorted by net funding. Prefer ideas where the funding pays me to hold the position.

This keeps the agent grounded in real OI, volume and funding rather than the idea list alone.
