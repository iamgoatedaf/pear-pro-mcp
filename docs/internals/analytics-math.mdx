`src/analytics.ts` implements the statistical-arbitrage helpers behind [`pear_pair_analytics`](/tools/local-quant). All inputs are aligned, equal-length price arrays ordered **oldest → newest**.

## Log returns

For a series $p$:

$$
r_i = \ln\!\left(\frac{p_i}{p_{i-1}}\right)
$$

Pairs where either price is ≤ 0 are skipped, so the return series can be slightly shorter than the price series.

## Mean, std, covariance

Sample statistics (default `sample = true`, dividing by $n-1$):

$$
\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n}\sum x_i, \quad
s = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x_i-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}, \quad
\operatorname{cov}(x,y) = \frac{\sum (x_i-\bar{x})(y_i-\bar{y})}{n-1}
$$

`std` returns `NaN` for fewer than 2 points; `mean` returns `NaN` for an empty array.

## Correlation

$$
\rho = \frac{\operatorname{cov}(x,y)}{s_x \, s_y}
$$

`correlationOfReturns` applies this to the legs' **log returns**.

## Beta (hedge ratio)

OLS hedge ratio of A on B, computed on returns:

$$
\beta = \frac{\operatorname{cov}(r_A, r_B)}{\operatorname{var}(r_B)}
$$

This is the weighting that makes the pair roughly market-neutral — short $\beta$ units of B per unit of A.

## Spread and z-score

The spread is the price ratio series:

$$
\text{spread}_i = \frac{p^A_i}{p^B_i}
$$

(points where $p^B_i = 0$ are skipped). The z-score compares the latest spread to a trailing window of size $w$:

$$
z = \frac{\text{spread}_{\text{last}} - \mu_w}{\sigma_w}
$$

`rollingZScore` applies the same formula at every index using a sliding window (emitting `NaN` until the window fills).

## Annualized volatility

$$
\sigma_{\text{ann}} = \sigma(r) \cdot \sqrt{\text{periodsPerYear}}
$$

`periodsPerYear` defaults to 365 (daily); use 8760 for hourly candles.

## Signal

```
z ≥ 2   → "extended_long_spread"
z ≤ -2  → "extended_short_spread"
else    → "neutral"
```

$|z| \ge 2$ is the conventional mean-reversion entry band.

## Defaults & guards

- `window` defaults to 30, clamped to the spread length.
- `rollingZScoreTail` returns the last 10 rolling z-scores.
- The tool requires `pricesA.length === pricesB.length` (≥3 each) and rejects mismatches before computing.
